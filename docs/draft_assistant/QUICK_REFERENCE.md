# Quick Reference: Document Evaluation Criteria

## Position Paper - 100 Points

| Category                              | Points | Key Elements                                                                   |
| ------------------------------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Structure & Organization**          | 20     | Header (Committee, Country, Delegate, Topic) • 5-7 paragraphs • Clear sections |
| **Country Representation & Accuracy** | 25     | Country mentions • Policy language • Diplomatic tone • National perspective    |
| **Research & Citations**              | 20     | 3-5+ citations • Statistics • UN/org references • Historical dates             |
| **Proposed Solutions & Feasibility**  | 20     | Action verbs • Implementation details • Concrete recommendations               |
| **Writing Quality & Diplomacy**       | 15     | 500-800 words • Formal tone • Good readability • No informal language          |

**Grades:**

- A (90-100%): Excellent - Professional MUN standard
- B (80-89%): Very Good - Strong with minor improvements needed
- C (70-79%): Good - Solid foundation, address key areas
- D (60-69%): Satisfactory - Needs significant improvement
- F (<60%): Needs Major Revision

## Working Paper - 100 Points

| Category                      | Points | Key Elements                                                       |
| ----------------------------- | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------ |
| **Format Compliance**         | 15     | Header • Sponsors/Signatories • Committee/Topic • Sections         |
| **Clause Structure**          | 20     | 3+ perambulatory clauses • 5+ operative clauses • Proper verbs     |
| **Comprehensiveness**         | 20     | 5+ numbered clauses • Multiple aspects covered • Broad scope       |
| **Feasibility & Specificity** | 25     | Actionable proposals • Timelines • Funding • Monitoring mechanisms |
| **Collaboration Evidence**    | 10     | Multiple sponsors • Collaborative language • Partnership emphasis  |
| **UN Principles Alignment**   | 10     | Human rights • Sovereignty • Peace • Development references        |

**Perambulatory Clause Verbs:** Noting, Recalling, Recognizing, Observing, Bearing in mind, Affirming, Deeply concerned, Emphasizing

**Operative Clause Verbs:** Recommends, Urges, Calls upon, Requests, Encourages, Supports, Establishes

## Draft Resolution - 100 Points

| Category                           | Points | Key Elements                                                         |
| ---------------------------------- | ------ | -------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Format & Structure**             | 15     | Complete header • Committee • Topic • Sponsors • Signatories         |
| **Preamble Quality**               | 20     | 5+ perambulatory clauses • Past UN resolutions • Treaties referenced |
| **Operative Clause Effectiveness** | 25     | 8+ operative clauses • Sub-clauses • Strong action verbs             |
| **Specificity & Implementation**   | 20     | Numbers/amounts • Timelines • Mechanisms • Monitoring systems        |
| **Diplomatic Language**            | 10     | Cooperative tone • No confrontational terms • Diplomatic verbs       |
| **UN Charter Compliance**          | 10     | Aligns with UN principles • International law • Human rights         |

**Format Rules:**

- Perambulatory clauses: Italicized, end with commas
- Operative clauses: Bold, numbered, end with semicolons (last ends with period)
- Sub-clauses: Lettered, indented

## Op-Ed - 100 Points

| Category                       | Points | Key Elements                                                        |
| ------------------------------ | ------ | ------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Compelling Opening**         | 15     | Strong hook • Headline • Question/statistic/quote/anecdote          |
| **Argument Strength & Logic**  | 25     | Clear thesis • Logical connectors • 5-8 paragraphs • Well-developed |
| **Evidence & Examples**        | 20     | Statistics • Concrete examples • Expert quotes • Studies cited      |
| **Persuasiveness**             | 20     | Emotional appeal • Call to action • Addresses counterarguments      |
| **Writing Style & Engagement** | 15     | 600-800 words • Readable (12-20 words/sentence) • Active voice      |
| **Country Perspective**        | 5      | Clear national viewpoint • Country interests represented            |

**Engaging Techniques:**

- Rhetorical questions
- Personal stories/anecdotes
- Powerful statistics
- Expert quotes
- Emotional language (crucial, vital, urgent)
- Direct address (we, you, our)

## Tips for High Scores

### All Documents

1. **Follow Format Exactly** - Formatting matters significantly
2. **Use Proper Terminology** - Diplomatic and UN-specific language
3. **Be Specific** - Concrete numbers, dates, mechanisms
4. **Cite Sources** - Always back up claims with evidence
5. **Proofread** - Grammar and spelling count

### Position Papers

- Strong country representation (mention country 3+ times)
- Include 5+ citations
- Use policy language (government, legislation, treaty, commitment)
- 500-800 words ideal

### Working Papers & Draft Resolutions

- Use correct clause verbs (see lists above)
- Include sub-clauses for detail
- Reference past UN resolutions
- Specify implementation (funding, timeline, monitoring)

### Op-Eds

- Start with a hook (question, statistic, story)
- Clear opinion/thesis
- Address counterarguments
- End with strong call to action
- 600-800 words

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

❌ **Informal language** (gonna, wanna, stuff, things)  
❌ **Confrontational terms** (must, demand, force)  
❌ **Vague proposals** (should try, might consider)  
❌ **No citations** (always include sources)  
❌ **Wrong format** (mixing document types)  
❌ **Too short/long** (follow word count guidelines)  
❌ **No country perspective** (represent your nation)  
❌ **Missing headers** (always include required info)
